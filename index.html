FAQ - Understanding Egg Prices

Why do egg prices fluctuate so much?

Egg prices change due to supply chain issues, inflation, avian flu outbreaks, and seasonal demand (e.g., Easter, holidays).

Where does this data come from?

Data is sourced from the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) and USDA reports. We use verified, official sources.

Why did eggs cost $9+ in some places but not others?

Local laws (e.g., cage-free mandates), regional shortages, and store markup policies affect pricing.

Will egg prices go down in 2025?

USDA predicts a drop, but it depends on flock recovery and economic conditions.
